Fixing one of Volkswagen's terrible designs:

As a cost-cutting measure pulled by VW engineers, the interior of the ID4 lacked physical buttons. On a single smooth piece of plastic, they managed to put 5 seperate touch capacitive buttons making it impossible to know where you are clicking without looking down. My creation clips on and adds ridges, making it easier to guide your finger when you're barreling down the highway at 60 mph.



Fixing ANOTHER one of Volkswagen's terrible designs:

This is for the headlight control. Just like the previous design, this would guide your fingers to the correct "button".



Fixing ANOTHER one of Volkswagen's terrible designs:

This is one of the worst parts of the car. On another single strip of smooth plastic, you have controls for the... are you ready? Radio power button, radio volume, driver's temperature, passenger temperature, driver's heated seats, and passenger's heated seats. Phew! I guess VW is allergic to buttons. My design creates borders between each seperate control, making it easier for your fingers to move to the correct section. (It doesn't help that you can also slide your finger across the plastic to adjust the volume/temperature)
